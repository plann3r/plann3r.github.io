Plann3r: Predicting Planning Costs Grounded in 3D

Abstract

Planning paths or trajectories for robot navigation requires understanding scene geometry and traversability. Classic approaches relied on accurate 3D maps to define occupancy based planning costs. Learning based methods predict planning costs either in terms of distance to goal or temporal distance between images. However, the former overfits to scene layout and the latter lacks geometric understanding. Moreover, these methods decouple planning and control by estimating scalar cost per image to rank actionable subgoals. In this paper, we propose Plann3r as a 3D grounded method to predict pixel-level planning cost for any given set of images with an arbitrary subgoal pixel. The resulting dense costmaps then directly condition a learnt control policy. We show that Plann3r outperforms search-based planning baselines that rely on explicit connectivity to obtain costmaps. Furthermore, we show that our predicted costmap-conditioned controller improves success rate across different navigation tasks.
